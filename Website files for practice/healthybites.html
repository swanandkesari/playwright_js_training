üí° Expert Nutrition Tips (role: region)

ü•§ Stay Hydrated Throughout the Day

The Science: Proper hydration supports metabolism, nutrient transport, and temperature regulation. Even mild dehydration can affect energy levels and cognitive function.

Practical Tip: Aim for 8-10 glasses of water daily. Start your morning with a glass of water, and keep a reusable bottle nearby. Add lemon, cucumber, or mint for variety.

Pro Tip: Monitor urine color - pale yellow indicates good hydration.

üåà Eat the Rainbow Daily

The Science: Different colored fruits and vegetables contain unique phytonutrients, antioxidants, and vitamins. Variety ensures comprehensive nutrition.

Practical Tip: Include at least 5 different colors in your daily meals. Red (tomatoes), orange (carrots), yellow (bell peppers), green (leafy vegetables), purple (eggplant).

Pro Tip: Frozen fruits and vegetables are just as nutritious as fresh and can be more convenient.

‚è∞ Practice Mindful Eating

The Science: Eating slowly allows your brain to register fullness signals (which take 15-20 minutes), leading to better portion control and digestion.

Practical Tip: Put your fork down between bites, chew thoroughly, and eliminate distractions like TV or phones during meals.

Pro Tip: Start meals with a moment of gratitude to promote mindful awareness.

ü•ú Include Healthy Fats Daily

The Science: Healthy fats support brain function, hormone production, and nutrient absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).

Practical Tip: Include avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish like salmon. Aim for 20-35% of daily calories from healthy fats.

Pro Tip: Add a tablespoon of ground flaxseed or chia seeds to smoothies for omega-3 fatty acids.
